Overview of Planning Processes

The "Plan" category in SCOR includes five key sub-processes that guide strategic and operational planning across the supply chain:

sP1 – Plan Supply Chain

Develop overall supply chain strategy, define performance targets, align resources, and establish business rules for planning and execution.

  • Strategic alignment
  • Supply chain configuration
  • Performance metrics

sP2 – Plan Source

Plan sourcing strategies, supplier relationships, and procurement policies to meet demand efficiently and cost-effectively.

  • Supplier capacity planning
  • Procurement scheduling
  • Risk and compliance planning

sP3 – Plan Make

Plan production activities, including capacity, scheduling, and resource allocation to meet demand and quality standards.

  • Production planning
  • Maintenance scheduling
  • Quality assurance planning

sP4 – Plan Deliver

Plan logistics, transportation, and distribution strategies to ensure timely and cost-effective delivery of products.

  • Warehouse and transport planning
  • Order fulfillment strategy
  • Customer service planning

sP5 – Plan Return

Plan reverse logistics processes including returns, repairs, recycling, and disposal to support sustainability and customer satisfaction.

  • Return policy planning
  • Repair and refurbishment strategy
  • Environmental compliance
